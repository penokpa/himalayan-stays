# Himalayan Stays — Business Plan

## What We Built

A hybrid PMS (Property Management System) + Booking Platform for Nepal's teahouse trekking lodges. Two apps:

1. **PMS/POS (Phone App)** — Lodge owners manage rooms, food orders, guest tabs, and daily sales. Works offline.
2. **Booking Platform (Website)** — Trekkers book multi-lodge itineraries online. Pay via Stripe, eSewa, or at the lodge.

---

## Business Model: The Hybrid Approach

### Phase 1 — Build Trust (Month 1-6)
- PMS/POS: **FREE forever**
- Booking platform: **FREE for lodges**
- Revenue: **NPR 0**
- Goal: Get 5-10 lodges actively using the PMS daily

### Phase 2 — Monetize Bookings (Month 6-12)
- Commission on **online bookings only**: **10%**
- Walk-in guests through PMS: **FREE** (no commission)
- Lodge owners keep 100% of customers they get themselves
- We only earn when we bring them NEW customers

### Phase 3 — Scale (Month 12-24)
- Agency partnerships: **5% referral fee** on agency bookings
- Premium lodge listings: **NPR 5,000/month** (appear first in search)
- Trekker insurance upsell: affiliate revenue
- Tourism data/analytics: B2G contracts with tourism board
- Expand to ABC, Langtang, Manaslu, Upper Mustang routes

### Revenue Example (Phase 2)
```
Trekker books 12-day EBC trek: NPR 26,000
├── Platform commission (10%):  NPR 2,600
└── Lodge receives (90%):       NPR 23,400
```

## The Moat (Why Competitors Can't Copy Easily)

No one else has **PMS + Booking combined**.

- Booking.com can list lodges but can't help owners manage rooms
- A PMS vendor can manage rooms but can't bring customers
- We do both — and the PMS data feeds real-time availability to the booking platform

The PMS is the trojan horse. Once lodges use it daily, we own the data — occupancy rates, pricing patterns, demand cycles. That data makes the booking platform accurate and trustworthy.

## How to Approach Lodge Owners

### The Pitch (One Sentence)
"I built something on the phone that replaces your register book. It tracks rooms, food orders, and daily sales. It's free."

### Who to Target First
- Lodges run by people you personally know
- Younger owners or owners' children (phone-comfortable)
- Lodges in Namche or Lukla (best internet, busiest)
- Mid-range lodges, NOT the biggest/fanciest first

### First 5 Targets (One Per Village)

| # | Village | Why This Stop |
|---|---------|---------------|
| 1 | Lukla | Gateway, busiest, best internet |
| 2 | Namche Bazaar | Acclimatization stop, 2-night stays, most competition |
| 3 | Tengboche | Every trekker stops here, near monastery |
| 4 | Dingboche | Second acclimatization stop, 2-night stays |
| 5 | Lobuche or Gorak Shep | High altitude = higher prices = more value from tracking |

### Onboarding Steps (Per Lodge)
1. Visit in person during off-season (less busy)
2. Show the PMS on YOUR phone with their actual rooms and menu
3. Set it up right there — add rooms, menu items, prices (5-10 minutes)
4. Stay for a day — help them check in first real guest
5. Check back in one week

### Common Objections & Responses

| They Say | You Say |
|----------|---------|
| "Paper works fine" | "It does. But at checkout do you ever forget a tea or dal bhat someone ordered 3 days ago?" |
| "I don't know phones" | "It's just tapping buttons — bigger than WhatsApp. Your son/daughter can help first day." |
| "What if internet goes?" | "It works without internet. Everything saves on the phone." |
| "What's the cost?" | "Free. I'm from here, I want our lodges to do better." |
| "Why are you doing this?" | "I want trekkers to book our lodges directly instead of paying 30% to Kathmandu agencies." |

### What NOT to Do
- Don't mention "platform" or "technology" or "SaaS"
- Don't talk about online bookings until Phase 2 (after PMS trust is built)
- Don't try to onboard more than 1-2 lodges per village visit
- Don't charge anything in Phase 1
- Don't target the biggest lodge first — start with friendliest

### Proving Value (After 1-2 Months of PMS Use)
Go back with their own data:
- "You sold NPR 45,000 in food last month — dal bhat was 40%"
- "Room 3 was empty 12 nights — that's NPR 18,000 lost revenue"
- "Your busiest day was Thursday — 8 tabs opened"

This is when they see their business clearly for the first time.

### Introducing Bookings (After 3-4 Months)
"Some trekkers want to book rooms before they arrive. If I send you guaranteed customers through the app, would you want that? I take 10% only on bookings I bring you. Walk-ins are still 100% yours."
